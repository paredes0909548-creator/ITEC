Easy

01. Street Tacos

Ingredients

  • Flank steak (Carne Asada) or boneless chicken thigh
  • Fresh nixtamal corn tortillas
  • White onion, cilantro, fresh radishes
  • Serrano peppers, Cotija cheese, limes

Procedure

  1. Sear the marinated carne asada over a high-heat cast-iron plancha or open flame to achieve a deep Maillard crust, maintaining a medium-rare interior before dicing against the grain.
  2. Using a molcajete, roughly crush fresh serranos and sea salt into a textured paste, then lightly incorporate the flesh of ripe Hass avocados and a splash of fresh lime juice.
  3. Flash-heat fresh nixtamal corn tortillas on a dry, hot comal until they puff slightly, indicating steam formation and proper internal hydration.
  4. Double-wrap the diced meat in warm tortillas, and top with a pristine, finely diced brunoise of white onion, finely chopped cilantro, sliced radishes, a sprinkle of salty Cotija cheese, and a lime wedge.
Easy

02. Northern Beef Burrito

Ingredients

  • Finely ground beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio)
  • Large, thin Sonoran flour tortillas
  • Refried pinto beans, Mexican red rice
  • Cumin, Mexican oregano, chipotle in adobo
  • Asadero or Chihuahua cheese, white onion

Procedure

  1. Sauté the finely diced onions in lard or vegetable oil until translucent, add the ground beef, and bloom the cumin, Mexican oregano, and salt directly into the rendered meat fat.
  2. Incorporate a tablespoon of chipotle in adobo purée and a small splash of beef stock, lowering the flame to let the liquid reduce until the beef mixture is thick, glossy, and intensely flavored.
  3. Steam a large, authentic Sonoran-style flour tortilla until fully pliable, then lay down a foundational spread of warm refried pinto beans and red rice across the center.
  4. Layer the seasoned beef and shredded Asadero cheese over the base; fold the outer vertical edges inward tightly, pull the bottom edge up and over the filling, and roll forward while maintaining tension. Sear seam-side down on a hot griddle until golden brown.
Easy

03. Enchiladas Rojas

Ingredients

  • Dried Guajillo and Ancho chilies
  • Shredded poached chicken breast
  • Stale corn tortillas, garlic, whole cloves
  • Queso Fresco, Mexican crema, pickled onions

Procedure

  1. Stem, seed, and toast dried Guajillo and Ancho chilies on a comal, then submerge them in boiling water for 15 minutes to rehydrate before blending with garlic, a pinch of clove, and poaching broth until perfectly smooth.
  2. Strain the red chili sauce through a fine-mesh chinois into a hot pot with a tablespoon of lard, simmering for 10 minutes to deepen the color and cook out raw vegetable notes.
  3. Lightly fry stale corn tortillas in hot oil for 5 seconds per side to gelatinize the starches, preventing them from tearing or turning mushy when submerged in the sauce.
  4. Dip each warm tortilla completely into the red sauce, stuff with shredded seasoned chicken, roll tightly into a cylinder, and place in a baking dish. Top with remaining sauce, crumbled Queso Fresco, a drizzle of crema, and pickled red onions.
Medium

04. Traditional Guacamole

Ingredients

  • Ripe Hass avocados
  • White onion, Serrano chilies
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Key limes, coarse sea salt

Procedure

  1. Grind finely minced white onion, sliced serrano chilies, and a generous pinch of coarse sea salt together in a volcanic rock molcajete until a vibrant, wet paste forms. This breaks the vegetable cell walls, releasing aromatic essential oils.
  2. Slice the Hass avocados in half, remove the pits, scoop the flesh into the molcajete, and use a pestle or fork to roughly mash, leaving distinct chunks for optimal mouthfeel.
  3. Gently fold in hand-torn cilantro leaves and a small squeeze of fresh lime juice, taking care not to over-mix, which can break down the unique texture into a smooth purée. Taste and adjust salt balance.
Medium

05. Pozole Rojo

Ingredients

  • Pork shoulder and pork shanks (with bone)
  • Nixtamalized dry hominy (Maíz Pozolero)
  • Dried Ancho and Guajillo chilies, garlic
  • Shredded cabbage, radishes, Mexican oregano

Procedure

  1. Submerge the pork shoulder and collagen-rich shanks in a large pot of cold water with half an onion and a head of garlic; bring to a boil, meticulously skimming off all impurities and scum to maintain a clean broth, then simmer for 2 hours.
  2. Rehydrate and purée the dried Ancho and Guajillo chilies with garlic, strain the paste directly into the pork broth, and add the rinsed nixtamalized hominy corn, simmering until the kernels bloom into "flowers."
  3. Extract the tender pork, shred it into bite-sized pieces, discard the bones, and return the meat back to the bubbling, aromatic red soup.
  4. Ladle the hot soup into wide bowls, allowing guests to garnish dynamically with shredded green cabbage, thinly sliced radishes, Mexican oregano crushed between their palms, and fresh lime juice.
Medium

06. Tamales de Pollo

Ingredients

  • Masa Harina (for tamales), rich chicken stock
  • Pork lard (whipped), baking powder
  • Salsa verde or shredded chicken filling
  • Dried corn husks (totomoxtle)

Procedure

  1. Soak dried corn husks in hot water for an hour to make them completely pliable. In a stand mixer, whip pork lard with baking powder until it mimics the light, airy consistency of meringue.
  2. Gradually alternate adding masa harina and warm chicken stock to the whipped lard, beating continuously until the dough is light; ensure a small dollop floats effortlessly in a glass of cold water.
  3. Pat a corn husk dry and spread 2 tablespoons of masa dough evenly across the smooth side of the husk, leaving a clean border around the sides and bottom edge.
  4. Place a spoonful of shredded chicken mixed with salsa verde down the center of the dough. Fold the sides of the husk inward so the masa seals the filling completely, fold the pointed tail up, and steam vertically in a tamalera for 60 to 90 minutes until the masa pulls away cleanly from the husk.
Medium

07. Mole Poblano

Ingredients

  • Chiles Mulato, Ancho, and Pasilla
  • Almonds, pumpkin seeds, raisins, plantains
  • Stale bolillo roll, Mexican chocolate (Abuelita)
  • Rich chicken stock, toasted sesame seeds

Procedure

  1. Separately fry dried Mulato, Ancho, and Pasilla chilies in hot lard, taking care not to scorch them, then steep them in hot chicken stock. In the same lard, fry almonds, pumpkin seeds, raisins, and sliced plantains until caramelized.
  2. Char a stale piece of bolillo bread and a corn tortilla to act as traditional binding agents. Blend all fried seeds, fruits, bread, and hydrated chilies with warm stock into an incredibly fine, velvety paste.
  3. Pour this complex paste into a heavy clay cazuela with hot lard, frying the paste while stirring constantly. Melt in discs of dark Mexican chocolate, adjusting sweetness and salt as the sauce thickens over a low flame.
  4. Simmer for 30 minutes until a glistening layer of oil separates on top, indicating the paste is properly fried. Spoon over roasted chicken pieces and garnish generously with toasted white sesame seeds.
Medium

08. Chiles Rellenos

Ingredients

  • Large, straight Poblano peppers
  • Queso Oaxaca or Queso Panela
  • Fresh eggs (separated), all-purpose flour
  • Charred tomato caldillo (sauce), frying oil

Procedure

  1. Blister Poblano peppers directly over an open gas flame until the skin is entirely charred, then immediately place them in a plastic bag to steam for 10 minutes, allowing the outer skin to slip off effortlessly.
  2. Carefully make a small longitudinal slit down the side of each pepper, peel away the internal seed pod without tearing the flesh, and stuff the cavity with shredded Queso Oaxaca.
  3. Whip room-temperature egg whites to stiff, glossy peaks, then gently fold the egg yolks back in one by one along with a pinch of flour to create a delicate, airy soufflé batter.
  4. Dust the stuffed Poblanos lightly with flour so the batter adheres, submerge them carefully into the egg batter, and fry in hot vegetable oil at 180°C (350°F) until puffed and golden brown. Serve nesting in a pool of hot, refined tomato caldillo.
Hard

09. Quesadillas de Flor de Calabaza

Ingredients

  • Freshly pressed uncooked masa discs
  • Fresh squash blossoms (Flor de calabaza)
  • Epazote leaves, white onion
  • Queso Oaxaca, lard or vegetable oil

Procedure

  1. Clean the delicate squash blossoms by removing the tough green sepals and the bitter internal pistils or stamens, then coarsely chop the remaining bright orange petals.
  2. Quickly sauté sliced white onions and the squash blossoms with a sprig of fresh epazote for 2 minutes over high heat to evaporate surface moisture while retaining the blossom's structural integrity.
  3. Press a ball of fresh corn masa into a flat disc using a tortilla press lined with plastic wrap. Lay strings of Queso Oaxaca and a spoonful of the blossom mixture onto one half of the raw masa.
  4. Using the plastic lining, fold the raw masa disc over the filling to create a half-moon shape, sealing the edges firmly with your fingers. Fry on a hot, lightly greased comal or shallow-fry in oil until the masa is cooked through, crisp, and the interior cheese is beautifully strung.
Hard

10. Arroz Rojo Classic

Ingredients

  • Long-grain white rice
  • Ripe tomatoes, white onion, garlic
  • Hot chicken stock, lard or neutral oil
  • Whole serrano pepper, sprig of fresh parsley

Procedure

  1. Soak long-grain white rice in hot water for 15 minutes, rinse thoroughly under cold water until the runoff is completely clear of surface starches, and drain until totally dry.
  2. Blend ripe tomatoes, onion, and garlic without adding excess water until a smooth purée forms, then strain to remove skins and seeds. Heat lard in a heavy pan and fry the dry rice grains, stirring constantly, until they turn an opaque, light golden hue and sound like sand shifting in the pan.
  3. Pour the tomato purée directly over the toasted rice; fry the rice *within* the tomato mixture for 3 to 4 minutes until the liquid reduces completely and the paste darkens, which eliminates raw vegetable notes.
  4. Stream in hot chicken stock, add a whole serrano pepper (slit slightly to release aroma without intense heat) and a sprig of parsley. Bring to a rolling boil, cover with a tight-fitting lid, lower heat to minimum, and simmer undisturbed for 15 minutes. Remove from heat, let it rest closed for 5 minutes, then fluff gently with a carving fork.
