Easy

01. Miso Soup

Ingredients

  • Dashi stock, Miso paste
  • Silken Tofu, Dried Wakame

Procedure

  1. Bring dashi stock to a gentle simmer, then drop in cubed silken tofu and dried wakame; cook for 2 minutes.
  2. Turn off the heat entirely. Ladle a small amount of warm dashi into a bowl to dissolve the miso paste smoothly, then stir it back into the pot before serving.
Easy

02. Chicken Teriyaki

Ingredients

  • Chicken thighs, Soy sauce
  • Mirin, Sake, Ginger

Procedure

  1. Prick chicken skin with a fork, then pan-fry skin-side down over medium-high heat until deep golden brown and crispy; flip and cook through.
  2. Pour out excess pan grease, add the mixture of soy sauce, mirin, sake, and grated ginger, and simmer until the sauce reduces into a thick, glossy glaze.
Easy

03. Udon Noodles

Ingredients

  • Udon, Dashi broth
  • Soy sauce, Scallions

Procedure

  1. Boil udon noodles in a large pot according to package instructions, immediately drain them, and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking.
  2. Heat dashi broth seasoned with soy sauce and mirin in a separate pot, place the reheated noodles into a serving bowl, pour the hot broth over, and garnish with sliced scallions.
Medium

04. Chicken Karaage

Ingredients

  • Chicken, Soy, Garlic, Ginger
  • Potato Starch (Katakuriko)

Procedure

  1. Marinate bite-sized chicken chunks in soy sauce, minced garlic, and ginger for 30 minutes, then dredge thoroughly in potato starch to create a light coat.
  2. Deep-fry at 160°C (320°F) for 3 minutes, rest out of the oil for 2 minutes, and flash-fry a second time at 190°C (375°F) for 1 minute until crisp and golden brown.
Medium

05. Pork Gyoza

Ingredients

  • Ground pork, Cabbage, Chives
  • Gyoza wrappers

Procedure

  1. Knead ground pork, minced wilted cabbage, and chives together, place a spoonful into a wrapper, wet the rim, and fold while pleating one side tightly shut.
  2. Sear flat-side down in a hot oiled skillet until brown, pour in 1/4 cup of water, cover instantly with a lid to steam for 5 minutes, then uncover to crisp the bottom.
Medium

06. Tonkatsu

Ingredients

  • Pork loin, Panko crumbs
  • Flour, Beaten egg

Procedure

  1. Pound pork loins to tenderize, score the fat edges to prevent curling, and season with salt and pepper before dusting with flour.
  2. Dip the pork completely into beaten egg, coat generously with airy panko crumbs, and deep-fry in hot oil at 170°C (340°F) for 5-6 minutes until cooked through.
Medium

07. Okonomiyaki

Ingredients

  • Cabbage, Flour, Dashi
  • Pork belly, Mayo, Seaweed

Procedure

  1. Whisk flour and dashi together, fold in plenty of finely shredded cabbage, pour onto a greased griddle to form a thick round, and drape pork belly slices over the top.
  2. Flip carefully after 5 minutes, cover to cook the pork through, then flip back and brush with okonomiyaki sauce, Japanese mayo, aonori seaweed, and bonito flakes.
Medium

08. Shrimp Tempura

Ingredients

  • Shrimp, Ice-cold water
  • Cake flour, Frying oil

Procedure

  1. Straighten peeled, deveined shrimp by making shallow incisions along the belly. Lightly mix cold cake flour with ice water, leaving small flour lumps intentionally.
  2. Dredge the shrimp in dry flour, dip into the cold batter, and immediately flash-fry in 180°C (350°F) oil for 2 minutes until light crisp and barely golden.
Hard

09. Shoyu Ramen

Ingredients

  • Broth bones, Ramen noodles
  • Soy base (Tare), Chashu pork

Procedure

  1. Simmer chicken bones, aromatics, and dashi over low heat for 6+ hours, skimming impurities regularly to yield a clear, robust stock.
  2. Pour 2 tablespoons of soy sauce tare into a heated bowl, mix with hot broth, drop in freshly boiled alkaline noodles, and arrange chashu pork and a soft-boiled egg on top.
Hard

10. Salmon Nigiri

Ingredients

  • Sushi-grade Salmon
  • Vinegared rice, Wasabi

Procedure

  1. Wet your hands with water, gently shape 20 grams of seasoned, warm sushi rice into a compact, unified oblong pod with your palm and fingers.
  2. Dab a tiny speck of wasabi onto a fresh, angled slice of sushi-grade salmon, drape the fish over the rice pod, and gently press the sides together to mold firmly.
