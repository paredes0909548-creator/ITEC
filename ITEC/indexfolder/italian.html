Kitchen Guide

A journey through 10 iconic regional classics. From the sun-drenched flavors of Sicily to the rich, buttery traditions of the North.

Easy

01. Classic Bruschetta

Ingredients

  • Ciabatta or rustic sourdough loaf
  • Ripe heirloom or San Marzano tomatoes
  • Garlic cloves, Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil, Sea salt

Procedure

  1. Core the tomatoes, slice them thinly, scrape away the wet seeds, and finely dice the remaining flesh before tossing it gently with hand-torn basil and premium extra virgin olive oil.
  2. Slice the bread into thick rounds, grill over an open flame or cast-iron skillet until dark char marks appear, and immediately scratch the rough surfaces with the cut side of a raw garlic clove.
  3. Spoon the seasoned tomato mixture high onto the warm, garlic-infused bread, allowing the juices to soak into the crumb, and finish with a final flake of sea salt.
Easy

02. Insalata Caprese

Ingredients

  • Mozzarella di Bufala (Campana DOP)
  • Ripe beefsteak or vine-ripened tomatoes
  • Freshly reduced balsamic vinegar glaze
  • Whole basil leaves, Finishing olive oil

Procedure

  1. Drain the fresh buffalo mozzarella from its brine and gently tear or slice it into thick disks, matching the size and thickness of your sliced tomatoes.
  2. Arrange the cheese and tomato slices in an overlapping, alternating fan pattern across a chilled serving plate, slotting a large, fresh basil leaf securely between each layer.
  3. Drizzle a generous thread of green finishing olive oil over the top, follow with a tight zigzag of balsamic glaze reduction, and season with cracked peppercorns.
Easy

03. Cacio e Pepe

Ingredients

  • Tonnarelli or heavy Spaghetti
  • Finely grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Whole black peppercorns, Salted water

Procedure

  1. Toast freshly crushed whole black peppercorns in a dry, heavy skillet over medium heat until highly aromatic, then instantly splash in a ladle of starchy pasta water to halt the toast.
  2. Boil your pasta in a shallow pot of lightly salted water to ensure a high concentration of starch, pulling it out 2 minutes before reaching *al dente* directly into the pepper pan.
  3. Remove the pan from the flame, let it cool slightly, then dump in the finely grated Pecorino Romano alongside a splash of cool pasta water, whipping vigorously to create a silky, emulsified cheese sauce.
Medium

04. Roman Carbonara

Ingredients

  • Cured pork jowl (Guanciale cubes)
  • Fresh egg yolks, 1 whole egg
  • Pecorino Romano, Rigatoni or Rigatoni-style pasta
  • Coarsely ground black pepper

Procedure

  1. Render cubed guanciale in a skillet over low heat until the fat pools and the meat becomes crispy and translucent, then separate the crisped pieces from the rendered fat.
  2. Whisk egg yolks and one whole egg with a mountain of finely grated Pecorino Romano and plenty of black pepper in a stainless steel bowl until a thick, golden paste forms.
  3. Toss hot pasta directly into the warm guanciale fat, remove completely from the stove, pour in the egg-and-cheese paste, and shake continuously with starchy water to build a smooth cream.
Medium

05. Pizza Margherita

Ingredients

  • Fermented Neapolitan pizza dough
  • Crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • Fresh Fior di Latte or Mozzarella slices
  • Fresh basil leaves, Extra virgin olive oil

Procedure

  1. Dust the fermented dough ball with semolina flour and gently press outwards from the center to create a raised outer rim (*cornicione*) without flattening the delicate air pockets.
  2. Spoon a thin layer of crushed San Marzano tomatoes across the base, scatter slices of fresh, drained mozzarella evenly, and lay down fresh basil leaves.
  3. Slide the pizza onto a blazing hot pizza stone or steel preheated to maximum temperature, baking until the crust swells, darkens into charred spots, and the cheese bubbles beautifully.
Medium

06. Eggplant Parmigiana

Ingredients

  • Italian eggplants, Rich tomato passata
  • Aged Parmigiano-Reggiano, Mozzarella
  • Coarse salt, Neutral oil for shallow frying

Procedure

  1. Slice eggplants lengthwise, layer them in a colander with coarse salt for an hour to extract bitter juices, then rinse thoroughly, pat completely dry, and shallow-fry until dark gold.
  2. Smear a thin coating of slow-simmered tomato passata at the bottom of a baking pan, then lay fried eggplant slices side-by-side to construct your baseline layer.
  3. Dust with grated Parmigiano-Reggiano, scatter torn mozzarella, and repeat the layering process before baking at 200°C (400°F) until the top sets into an intensely caramelized crust.
Medium

07. Sicilian Arancini

Ingredients

  • Chilled saffron-infused risotto
  • Slow-cooked beef ragù with peas
  • Mozzarella cubes, Fine breadcrumbs
  • Flour-and-water batter, Frying oil

Procedure

  1. Take a handful of chilled, starchy saffron risotto, flatten it in your palm, and hollow out a pocket to place a spoonful of thick meat ragù and a single cube of mozzarella.
  2. Gather the rice over the core filling and shape it into a tight cone or sphere, taking care to seal any cracks where moisture might escape during cooking.
  3. Dredge the ball through a light, thin flour-and-water batter, roll it thoroughly in fine breadcrumbs, and deep-fry in clean hot oil until beautifully crisp and melt-in-the-center hot.
Medium

08. Tiramisu

Ingredients

  • Savoiardi ladyfingers, Creamy Mascarpone
  • Fresh eggs, Espresso coffee
  • Unsweetened Dutch cocoa powder, Marsala wine

Procedure

  1. Whip egg yolks and sugar over a gentle double boiler (*bagnomaria*) until pale and thick, cool slightly, then fold smoothly into softened mascarpone cheese alongside stiffly beaten egg whites.
  2. Quickly submerge Savoiardi ladyfingers into a shallow bowl of cold, strong espresso spiked with Marsala wine, turning them once so they absorb liquid without turning mushy.
  3. Layer the soaked cookies tightly in a dish, spread a generous blanket of the mascarpone cream over them, repeat the stack, and chill for 6 hours before dusting heavily with Dutch cocoa powder.
Hard

09. Lasagna Bolognese

Ingredients

  • Fresh egg pasta sheets, Whole milk
  • Minced beef and pork, Butter, Flour
  • Carrots, Celery, Onions (*soffritto*), White wine
  • Parmigiano-Reggiano

Procedure

  1. Build a deep flavorsome base by sweating finely minced carrots, celery, and onions (*soffritto*) in butter, adding mixed meats to brown deeply, splashing with white wine, and low-simmering with milk and tomato for 3 hours.
  2. Whisk melted butter and flour over medium heat to cook out the raw flour taste, then slowly stream in warm whole milk to create a velvety, lump-free béchamel sauce.
  3. Assemble by layering blanched pasta sheets, rich ragù, and smooth béchamel, dusting every single layer with Parmigiano-Reggiano before baking until bubbly.
Hard

10. Risotto Milanese

Ingredients

  • Arborio or Carnaroli rice, Saffron threads
  • Dry white wine, Simmering bone marrow stock
  • Cold butter, Parmigiano-Reggiano, Shallots

Procedure

  1. Toast Carnaroli rice in a pan with sweated shallots until the grains are translucent, then pour in dry white wine and let it completely evaporate away.
  2. Begin adding simmering, saffron-infused bone marrow stock one ladle at a time, stirring constantly to scratch the rice grains together and release their starches into the liquid.
  3. Once the rice is *al dente*, remove from heat and vigorously whip in cubes of freezing cold butter and grated cheese (*mantecatura*) until the risotto is creamy and waves smoothly (*all'onda*).
