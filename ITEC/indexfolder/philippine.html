A step-by-step masterclass in 10 iconic recipes. From chicken adobo, sinigang, to crispy lechon, kare-kare, sisig and other savory dishes of the Philippines.

Easy

01. Pancit Bihon

Ingredients

  • Bihon noodles, Chicken or Pork strips
  • Garlic, Onion, Carrots, Cabbage
  • Soy sauce, Chicken broth, Calamansi

Procedure

  1. Sauté minced garlic and chopped onion until translucent, add meat strips, and cook until completely browned.
  2. Pour in soy sauce and broth, bring to a boil, then toss in sliced carrots and cabbage to cook for 2 minutes before transferring the vegetables to a side plate.
  3. Submerge the dry bihon noodles into the remaining boiling broth, stir continuously until they absorb all the liquid and become tender, then fold the vegetables back in and serve with fresh calamansi halves.
Easy

02. Lumpia Shanghai

Ingredients

  • Ground Pork, Carrots, Onion, Garlic
  • Egg, Salt, Black pepper
  • Lumpia wrappers, Frying oil

Procedure

  1. Combine ground pork, finely minced carrots, onions, garlic, egg, salt, and pepper in a bowl, mixing thoroughly until a sticky paste forms.
  2. Lay a lumpia wrapper flat, place 1-2 tablespoons of filling along the edge, roll tightly while tucking the sides, and seal the tip with a dab of water or egg white.
  3. Deep-fry the rolls in medium-hot oil until the wrappers turn an even, golden brown and the meat center registers fully cooked.
Easy

03. Sinangag with Egg

Ingredients

  • Day-old cooked rice, Head of garlic
  • Eggs, Salt, Cooking oil

Procedure

  1. Sauté a generous amount of finely crushed garlic in oil over low-medium heat until it turns a deep, crunchy golden brown, then drain half to use as a topping.
  2. Crumble cold, day-old rice into the pan, toss it vigorously to coat each grain in the aromatic garlic oil, season with salt, and pan-fry for 5-8 minutes.
  3. Fry eggs to your preference in a separate pan, lay them over a hot mound of garlic rice, and dust with the reserved crispy garlic bits.
Medium

04. Chicken Adobo

Ingredients

  • Chicken pieces, Soy sauce, Cane vinegar
  • Garlic, Bay leaves, Whole peppercorns
  • Water, Cooking oil

Procedure

  1. Simmer chicken pieces directly in soy sauce, vinegar, crushed garlic, bay leaves, and whole peppercorns over medium heat for 20 minutes without stirring initially to let the vinegar settle.
  2. Separate the chicken pieces from the braising liquid, pat dry, and quickly pan-sear them in a hot oiled skillet until the skins develop a caramelized crust.
  3. Pour the remaining adobo sauce back into the skillet with the chicken, reduce the liquid until it thickens into a rich glaze, and serve over steamed white rice.
Medium

05. Sinigang na Baboy

Ingredients

  • Pork belly, Tamarind base, Radish
  • Kangkong, Sitaw, Eggplant, Gabi
  • Onion, Tomato, Fish sauce, Water

Procedure

  1. Boil water with quartered tomatoes, onions, and taro cubes (*gabi*) until the taro begins to soften and thicken the liquid.
  2. Add the pork belly chunks and simmer on low heat for 40 minutes until melt-in-your-mouth tender, skimming any surface froth as it rises.
  3. Stir in the tamarind base, add string beans (*sitaw*), sliced radish, and eggplant, cook for 5 minutes, then turn off the heat and stir in the *kangkong* leaves to wilt under the residual steam.
Medium

06. Beef Kaldereta

Ingredients

  • Beef brisket, Tomato sauce, Liver spread
  • Potatoes, Carrots, Bell peppers
  • Garlic, Onion, Chili peppers, Cheese

Procedure

  1. Sear cubed beef brisket in a hot pot until browned, then sauté with garlic, onions, and chopped chili peppers until highly fragrant.
  2. Pour in tomato sauce and water, cover, and braise on low heat for roughly 1 to 1.5 hours until the beef fibers pull apart easily.
  3. Stir in the liver spread and grated cheese to build a creamy consistency, add the potato and carrot chunks to simmer until soft, then finish with crisp bell peppers.
Medium

07. Pork Sisig

Ingredients

  • Pork mask/ear, Chicken livers
  • Red onion, Siling labuyo, Calamansi
  • Soy sauce, Mayonnaise, Butter

Procedure

  1. Boil the pork mask until tender, char it thoroughly over a charcoal grill to achieve a smoky aroma, and chop both the pork and boiled chicken livers into fine, uniform pieces.
  2. Melt butter in a skillet, throw in the chopped meat mixture, and pan-fry on high heat until dry and intensely crispy.
  3. Toss in plenty of finely diced raw red onions and birds eye chilies (*siling labuyo*), splash with soy sauce and calamansi juice, and stir in mayonnaise before serving on a screaming hot plate.
Hard

08. Chicken Inasal

Ingredients

  • Chicken leg quarters, Lemongrass, Ginger
  • Garlic, Calamansi juice, Coconut vinegar
  • Annatto seeds, Butter or Oil

Procedure

  1. Marinate chicken pieces for at least 4 hours in a mixture of smashed lemongrass, ginger, garlic, calamansi juice, coconut vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  2. Infuse warm oil or melted butter with annatto seeds over low heat until it bleeds a vibrant, deep orange hue, then strain to create your basting oil.
  3. Grill the chicken over hot charcoal, basting continuously with the annatto oil every few minutes until beautifully charred, glossy, and cooked to the bone.
Hard

09. Crispy Lechon Belly

Ingredients

  • Slab of pork belly, Lemongrass stalks
  • Garlic heads, Red onions, Scallions
  • Salt, Black pepper, Milk (for brushing)

Procedure

  1. Score the inside flesh of a large pork belly slab, rub generously with salt and cracked black pepper, and pack the center tightly with crushed lemongrass stalks, garlic, onions, and scallions.
  2. Roll the belly into a secure cylinder, tie it tightly with kitchen twine at 1-inch intervals, and prick the outer skin entirely with a needle to encourage blistering.
  3. Brush the skin lightly with milk or oil, and slow-roast at 160°C (320°F) for 2 hours, then crank the heat up to 220°C (425°F) for the last 30 minutes until the skin shatters when tapped.
Hard

10. Kare-Kare

Ingredients

  • Oxtail, Beef tripe, Peanut butter
  • Banana heart, Eggplant, String beans
  • Annatto seeds, Garlic, Bagoong alamang

Procedure

  1. Simmer oxtail and tripe in a deep pot for 2-3 hours until gelatinous and tender, saving the rich meat broth for your sauce base.
  2. Sauté garlic and onions, pour in the reserved meat broth mixed with smooth peanut butter and warm annatto water (for color), stirring constantly until it reaches a thick consistency.
  3. Blanch the banana heart slices, string beans, and eggplants separately to preserve color, arrange them alongside the meat in the peanut sauce, and serve paired with sautéed shrimp paste (*bagoong alamang*).
